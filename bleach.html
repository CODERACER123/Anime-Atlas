Bleach

Ichigo Kurosaki gains Soul Reaper powers and fights Hollows while protecting both the living world and the Soul Society.

Suggested rating: TV-14 Suggested age: 14+

Core Themes

  • Duty and protection
  • Power growth and identity
  • Friendship under pressure

Story overview

Spiritual realms, sword battles, and evolving powers.

Act 1: Soul Reaper beginnings

Ichigo meets Rukia and inherits her powers, starting his journey as a substitute Soul Reaper.

Act 2: Soul Society rescue

Ichigo and friends infiltrate Soul Society to save Rukia, facing captains and new allies.

Act 3: Arrancar conflict

The fight expands with Arrancar and the threat of Aizen’s plans, pushing Ichigo’s limits.

Finale: Thousand-Year Blood War

The Quincy invasion reveals hidden truths and forces Soul Society into its greatest battle.

Fun facts

Highlights from the world of Soul Reapers.

Zanpakutō swords have unique spirits.

Each Soul Reaper’s blade reflects their personality and powers.

Bleach popularized "Bankai".

The term became iconic for powered-up transformations.

Style is a hallmark of the series.

Character designs and fashion heavily influenced its look.

Notable arcs

  • Soul Society
  • Arrancar
  • Fullbring
  • Thousand-Year Blood War

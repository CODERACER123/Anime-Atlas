One Punch Man

Saitama is so powerful he ends every fight with a single punch, which leaves him bored as he searches for a real challenge.

Suggested rating: TV-14 Suggested age: 14+

Core Themes

  • Absurd power and boredom
  • Hero society and reputation
  • Comedy with epic action

Story overview

A hero who wins too easily.

Act 1: The caped baldy

Saitama becomes a hero for fun and meets Genos, who vows to become his disciple after witnessing his strength.

Act 2: Hero Association trials

Saitama joins the Hero Association, but his low ranking clashes with his unmatched power.

Act 3: Monsters and rivals

Threats like the Sea King and the Monster Association expose how hero society struggles without Saitama’s spotlight.

Finale: Searching for meaning

Saitama continues to seek purpose, friendship, and recognition despite his overwhelming strength.

Fun facts

Trivia from the world of heroes.

The series began as a webcomic.

Creator ONE originally published it online before it became a manga and anime.

Saitama’s training is simple.

His routine is intentionally plain: push-ups, sit-ups, squats, and a 10km run.

It parodies shōnen tropes.

One Punch Man flips typical power progression on its head.
